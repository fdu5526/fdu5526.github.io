Loop Subdivision

What Is It?

Loop subdivision is a technique in computer graphics used to "smooth" a 3D mesh by taking its triangles and making more triangles out of them. This results in a rounder and higher quality look. This is the 2nd project of Carnegie Mellon's Computer Graphics course (15-462) and is written within 2 weeks.

What Is It Written In?

The loop subdivison algorithm is written in C++ using the OpenGL.

What Is the Algorithm?

For each triangle in the mesh, we divide it up into 4 smaller triangles. However, for each triangle in the mesh, we need to keep track of its 3 vertices, its normal vector, and its texture coordinates. These all need to be recomputed with each round of subdivision, causing the amount of data will quadruple. Managing this large amount of data was the biggest challange.

What Did I Learn?

My most important takeaway from this assignment was how to manage large amounts of data in multiple data structures efficiently. I also learned texture mapping and of course, the subdivision algorithm itself.
