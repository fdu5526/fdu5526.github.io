The Curious Tale of an Unexpected Visit

Comedic Narrative Game about Destroying Cleaning a Victorian Living Room

Fall 2016

What Is It?

The Curious Tale of an Unexpected Visit is a 2D sidescrolling comedic narrative game made in Unity. All of the visuals of its collage art style come from internet archives. I worked in a team of 2 over the course of a semester for Richard Lemarchand's game production class. The game typically takes 5-10 minutes to play through.



Trailer for the game

What Is It About??

One day in the Victorian era, the player character, a steampunk maid robot named Charlie, is commanded by its owner Amelia to clean up her terribly messy living room due to a sudden unexpected visit from a childhood friend, Ferdinand. As she holds him off at the door with awkward banter, the player must frantically clean up the living room via any means necessary.



"Any means necessary" includes throwing out a scandalous letter with its typewriter straight out of the window.

What Did I Do?

I worked as the team's lead programmer and shared the designer role with my teammate.

To create a better workflow for narrative design process, I wrote a parser and interpreter that read my own custom programming language from text files and transform it into narrative events. Because of this setup, we could very quickly iterate on anything from dialogues to visual effects and scripted events by simply editing easy-to-read text documents.

I also designed and programmed a narrative system that generates massive amounts of story paths via combinatorics. The game system achieves this by remembering every player action, such as how one chose to get rid of an item if at all, and dealing appropriate narrative consequences at the right moments.
