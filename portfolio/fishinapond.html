Fish in a Pond

Fish That Learns Music From You

What Is It?

The user teaches a fish that lives in a pond to play the tunes he or she likes via the stars in the sky and the reflections in the water. This is the final project of CMU's Electronic Media Studio II and is written within 2 weeks.

How does it Work?

The user can either choose to let the fish play a tune, or play a tune to the fish via a MIDI keyboard. After the tune is played, the user votes whether it is likable or unlikable. The fish will then learn via a machine learning algorithm and adjust the tunes it plays in the future to the user's preferences.

How was it Made?

The project is written in Processing, the graphics are made in Adobe Photoshop. The libraries I used are Wekaizing (which uses Weka as its backend) for machine learning and RWMidi for MIDI keyboard input support. I also used sound effects from freesound.org. Also special thanks to Professor Richard Randall and Professor Golan Levin at CMU for advising me through this project.

What Is the Purpose?

My research professor has a project called Simstudent (simstudent.org) where a human student teaches a computer "Simstudent" algebra so the human student can learn via teaching. I found great joy in teaching it to slowly become a math genius. I wish to create the same emotion in my users as they raise some silly fish to becoming able to play tunes that he or she likes to hear.
